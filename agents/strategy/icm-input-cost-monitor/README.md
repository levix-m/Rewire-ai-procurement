# ICM (Input Cost Monitor)

ICM tracks **input cost drivers**—resins, metals, freight indices, wage benchmarks—and converts market moves into **category impact** language procurement can act on.

## Why it matters

Markets move faster than category plans. ICM gives teams a **shared fact base** for pass-through discussions, should-cost refreshes, and timing of RFx waves.

## Common inputs

- Published indices (commodity, freight, FX)
- Should-cost models or simplified BOM weights
- Contractual pass-through formulas and caps

## Typical outputs

- Heatmap: which categories are most exposed this quarter
- Scenario table: if index moves X%, expect Y impact band on landed cost
- Brief for supplier conversations with **assumption ledger**

## Workflow in practice

Monthly **commodity council** prep; ad hoc spikes during shocks.

## Extension opportunities

- Hook to **Rapid Repricing** for in-session scenario math
- Auto-generate **clause-aware** recovery options (non-legal)

## Differentiation

- **SpendSculptor** fixes historical data representation.
- **ICM** explains **external driver** trajectories and **forward exposure**.
