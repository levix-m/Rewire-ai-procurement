# Source AI

Source AI focuses on **sourcing strategy** and **opportunity identification**—the decisions that happen *before* documents go out and suppliers engage. It connects business goals, spend reality, and market context into **credible event hypotheses**.

## Why it matters

Running RFx without a strategy burns supplier goodwill and internal time. Source AI helps teams choose **when** to compete, **what** to bundle, **who** belongs on a long list, and **which risks** need mitigation before negotiations start.

## Common inputs

- Category objectives (cost, resilience, innovation, ESG)
- Spendscape-style baseline or equivalent cube
- Market signals: commodity trend, capacity rumors, regulatory changes

## Typical outputs

- 2–4 sourcing strategy options with pros/cons
- Recommended event sequence (RFI → RFP, multi-lot, regional waves)
- Long-list hypotheses with objective inclusion/exclusion criteria

## Workflow in practice

Use after **Spendscape** (or parallel if strategy is hypothesis-driven) and upstream of **S4G** / **eRFX Agent** execution.

## Extension opportunities

- Tie long-list generation to **sanctions** and **diversity** policies via tool calls
- Auto-link strategy bullets to **RFX Creator** section outlines
- Score strategies against **scenario stress** (FX, logistics shocks)

## Differentiation

- **Spendscape** answers “what does spend look like?”
- **Source AI** answers “how should we go to market given that landscape?”
