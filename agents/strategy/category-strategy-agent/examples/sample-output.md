# Sample output — Category Strategy Agent

## Strategic context

Travel is both **growth-enabling** and **policy-leaky**. Strategy must tighten **channel control** while preserving **sales agility**—especially for customer-facing roles.

## Objectives & principles

1. **Controlled choice:** preferred channels + exceptions with audit trail
2. **Experience parity:** reduce friction vs consumer sites via inventory and policy design
3. **Sustainability accountability:** measurable emissions reporting by cost center

## Demand & supply levers

| Theme | Demand levers | Supply levers |
|-------|---------------|---------------|
| Visibility | Mandated booking tool for reimbursed trips | Consolidate TMC; harmonize data feed |
| Cost | Tiered policy by role/region | Renegotiate TMC fees; optimize payment |
| Risk | Pre-trip approval for high-risk locales | Security-approved routing |

## Roadmap (illustrative)

- **0–6 mo:** Data unify; baseline leakage; interim policy patch
- **6–12 mo:** Dual TMC down-select; pilot in NA + EU
- **12–24 mo:** Global rollout; emissions dashboard in monthly ops review

## KPI tree (abbreviated)

- **Leakage rate** → realized negotiated savings vs potential
- **Channel compliance** → incident rate & audit findings
- **Traveler NPS** → retention proxy for policy acceptance
- **kg CO₂e / $ travel** → sustainability trajectory

## Governance

- **Monthly** category ops with TMC QBRs
- **Quarterly** steering with CFO + sales COO on policy changes
