# Spendscape

Spendscape turns **disparate transactions** into a **strategic landscape**—where money flows, where suppliers concentrate risk, and where credible sourcing opportunities hide before anyone drafts an RFx.

## Why it matters

Category strategies fail when teams debate numbers instead of decisions. Spendscape aligns everyone on **one defensible view** of spend, concentration, and leakage—then frames opportunities with explicit **confidence** and **data limits**.

## Common inputs

- Rolling 12–36 month spend with supplier keys, sites, and cost centers
- Parent/child supplier roll-ups and JV aliases
- Contract effective coverage vs spend (even rough flags help)

## Typical outputs

- Heatmaps: category × region × supplier concentration
- Opportunity thesis list (consolidation, specification harmonization, demand management)
- Executive storyline for QBRs with “known unknowns” called out

## Workflow in practice

Use Spendscape **before** major sourcing waves and **after** taxonomy or M&A changes. Pair with **SpendSculptor** when raw data needs hygiene first.

## Extension opportunities

- Embed sustainability or risk overlays (ESG, geopolitical) as optional layers
- Push “landscape snapshots” to a data product consumed by BI tools
- Add scenario cuts for inflation-indexed baselines when paired with ICM

## Related agents

- **SpendSculptor** — when visibility is blocked by messy data
- **Source AI** — when opportunities should become sourcing hypotheses
