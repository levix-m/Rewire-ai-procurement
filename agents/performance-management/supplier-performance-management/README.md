# Supplier Performance Management

**Supplier Performance Management (SPM)** is the **governance agent**: it designs and runs the **operating cadence**—reviews, corrective actions, issue ownership, and escalations—so supplier relationships do not drift on anecdotes.

## Why it matters

Dashboards alone do not change behavior. SPM ensures **actions**, **accountability**, and **time-bound recovery** when performance misses contractual or operational expectations.

## Common inputs

- Contractual SLAs and scorecard definitions
- Incident history (quality holds, delivery failures, responsiveness)
- Business priorities: which failures are existential vs tolerable short term

## Typical outputs

- Quarterly business review storyline with **decisions**, not only charts
- Corrective action plans with milestones and exit criteria
- Escalation memos when governance forums must intervene

## Workflow in practice

Pair with **Supplier Performance Cockpit** for visibility: cockpit shows *what*; SPM drives *so what* and *now what*.

## Extension opportunities

- Auto-create CAPA tasks in ITSM from recurring KPI breaches
- Link remediation to **Negotiation Coach** when commercial levers are needed

## Differentiation

- **Supplier Performance Cockpit** emphasizes **monitoring and insight**.
- **SPM** emphasizes **governance, actions, and cadence**.
